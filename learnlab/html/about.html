ABOUT US




We are a nonprofit organization that specializes in codesign research between education technology companies and schools. We match parents, learners and educators with edtech developers to inform, develop, and evaluate the next generation of classroom tools. We study how well edtech tools work in real classroom environments, and connect promising edtech solutions with resources to support accelerating their impact.

OUR WHY




As we emerge from the COVID-19 pandemic, we find an education landscape dramatically changed. When the onset of the pandemic forced most schools to close their doors and move to distance learning, the appetite for remote, virtual, and technologically-enabled learning solutions (edtech) skyrocketed. In fact, edtech investment in the United States grew from $2.2 billion raised in 2020 to $8.2 billion by 2021, a sharp increase from what had been modest gains over the last five years. Yet, even with increased investment, spending, and interest in edtech, the overall effectiveness of edtech on learning remains uncertain.


Traditional research methods are too long, expensive, and cumbersome to keep pace with the rapid rate of edtech development and classroom adoption. At the same time, educational inequities that disproportionately impact black, latinx, and indigenous communities have been exacerbated by pandemic induced learning loss, with some estimates projecting that “students of color could be six to 12 months behind, compared with four to eight months for white students.” This leaves us with a conundrum—how can technology live up to its potential of being a great equalizer if we don’t understand which edtech tools work best, under what conditions and for whom?


And yet, herein also lies an opportunity⁠—what is possible for the sector if we are able to usher in a new generation of transformational learning tools that are developed and evaluated by, for, and with the diverse set of educators and learners they seek to serve? ⁠
